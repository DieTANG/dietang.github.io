Research Interests

Computational Regulatome

Post-transcriptional regulation is critical in determing cellular phenotypes and behavior. I am currently combining computational methods to identify the cell-type-specific RNA regulatory code underlying development and disease.

Potato Genomics

Genome evolution and diversity of wild and cultivated potatoes (Tang et al., Nature, 2022) Paper Link
Pan-potato Database

  • High-quality of 44 diploid wild and cultivated potato genomes.
  • Extensive phylogenomic relationships suggest the complexity of potato evolution.
  • Potato expanded its repetoire of disease-resistance genes, indicative of the effect of tuber-based propagation strategies on the evolution of the potato genome.
  • Multi-omics methods identified a transcription factor that determines tuber identity and interacts with the mobile tuberization inductive signal SP6A.
  • A map of large inversions provides insights for improving inbred lines and precluding potential linkage drag.

Genome design of hybrid potatoes (Zhang et al., Cell, 2021) Paper Link
PiGBS pipeline

  • Genetic analysis facilitates purging of deleterious mutations
  • Breaking tight linkage of deleterious mutations is required to develop elite lines
  • Crossing of inbred lines from different lineages generated vigorous F1 hybrids

Haplotype-resolved genome analyses of a heterozygous diploid potato (Zhou et al., Nature Genetics, 2020) Paper Link
Code in this study

  • Comparison of the two haplotypes revealed ~2.1% intragenomic diversity
  • Deleterious mutations and differentially expressed alleles were dispersed throughout both haplotypes

The genetics basis of inbreeding depression in potato (Zhang et al., Nature Genetics, 2019) Paper Link
PiGBS pipeline

  • The deleterious mutations in potato are enriched in the pericentrometic regions and are line specific
  • Using three F2 populations, we identified 15 genomic regions with severe segregation distortions due to selection at the gametic and zygotic stages
  • Most of the deleterious recessive alleles affecting survival and growth vigor were located in regions with high recombination rates
